🪞 Reflection & Growth

💡 What Went Well

  • Delivered an end-to-end automation bot in Power Automate that solved a real-world procurement bottleneck.
  • Produced a documentary-style townhall video seen by the entire organisation — from scripting to filming and editing.
  • Tested and evaluated Microsoft 365 Copilot with prompt engineering, tackling hallucinations and user scenarios.
  • Got to work directly with zookeepers and Park Ops teams, offering empathy-driven business process optimisation solutions.
  • Balanced technical development with stakeholder engagement and executive-level presentations.

🤔 Challenges Faced

  • Balancing multiple major deliverables (RPA bot development, video production, HR testing) under tight timelines.
  • Overcoming filming constraints like poor lighting, ambient noise, and camera shyness during interviews.
  • Debugging Microsoft Forms permission issues and working around Power Automate quirks without much documentation.
  • Initially hesitant to seek clarification, which slowed down onboarding in the first two weeks.
  • Learning to communicate technical ideas to non-technical stakeholders required patience and simplification.

📘 Summary of My Internship

My internship with Mandai’s Transformation Office was nothing short of transformative. It offered me a rare opportunity to work across technical automation, creative media production, and real-world workflow redesign — all within a mission-driven environment focused on conservation and wellbeing.

From day one, I was trusted with responsibilities that challenged me to step out of my comfort zone — whether it was automating procurement processes using RPA or scripting interviews for a company-wide townhall video. Each week came with new domains, new departments, and new expectations which pushed me to adapt quickly and learn independently.

I collaborated with zookeepers, HR teams, procurement staff, and even technology partners to understand pain points and design solutions that made a difference. It wasn’t just a technical role; it was a people-first experience that showed me the value of empathy in digital transformation.

So far, I have developed a full working RPA bot, presented work at a townhall, tested GenAI tools, and built a multi-page digital portfolio — a true culmination of all the skills I had cultivated. I have not only become more skilled, but more assured of the kind of impact I want to create in the world.

🧠 Technical Learnings

On the technical front, this internship gave me robust exposure to low-code platforms, automation frameworks, and AI experimentation. I worked extensively with Microsoft Power Automate Desktop to build RPA bots that handled file downloads, exception handling, SharePoint uploads, and dynamic naming conventions. I learned to fine-tune selectors, configure retry logic, and structure workflows modularly.

Additionally, I explored prompt engineering within Microsoft Copilot 365, crafting scenarios to test hallucinations, tune outputs, and create role-specific question templates. I also dealt with permission bugs in SharePoint Forms and implemented domain-wide access solutions — improving my understanding of Microsoft ecosystems and security layers.

Outside automation, I dabbled in data visualisation using Excel PivotTables, dashboard logic using Power BI, and conducted experiments with GenAI platforms like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot. I analysed platform bias and brand visibility to extract tangible business insights.

I also deepened my ability to communicate technical ideas to non-technical stakeholders — using diagrams, visuals, and simplified walkthroughs. Whether it was building flowcharts in Miro, creating media edits in Filmora, or testing AI integrations — I walked away with a stronger, more practical toolkit to build real-world impact.

🌱 My Experience at Mandai

What stood out most during my internship at Mandai Wildlife Group was not just the technology, tools, or workflows — it was the deep-rooted culture of humility, mission, and people-first thinking. From the moment I joined, I was welcomed into a team that prioritised collaboration, mutual respect, and continuous learning. I wasn’t seen as “just an intern,” but as a full contributor trusted with real projects and real outcomes.

Working alongside zookeepers, HR personnel, procurement staff, and senior innovation leads gave me an incredibly diverse exposure to how conservation and operational excellence intersect. The fieldwork I did during the DILO (Day-in-the-Life) observations was humbling — I scrubbed floors, prepped food, lifted buckets, and saw firsthand the emotional and physical toll that animal care teams endure. Yet, not once did I feel out of place. Everyone I interacted with — from the South Zone Herbivore Team to transformation stakeholders took the time to explain, include, and support me.

One of the biggest cultural takeaways for me was Mandai’s ability to blend tradition with innovation. Despite its legacy as a beloved wildlife institution, the group is embracing AI, automation, and digital tools to stay future-ready — and I was right in the middle of it. I got to build bots that genuinely saved staff time, design visuals that simplified communication, and even script a story that captured organisational values in a townhall video. I saw how even small tech-driven improvements could have ripple effects across wellbeing, time management, and guest experience.

What I’ll carry forward from this internship isn’t just the skills or portfolio pieces — it’s a mindset. I’ve learned to lead with empathy, to stay curious, and to always understand the human impact behind every workflow or codebase I touch. I’ve seen how technology, when grounded in compassion and clarity, can uplift not just operations, but people’s everyday lives.

In short, Mandai didn’t just teach me how to be a better intern — it taught me how to be a more thoughtful and impactful innovator. And for that, I will always be grateful.
