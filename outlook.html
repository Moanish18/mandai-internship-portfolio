🌿 Outlook & Aspirations

🧰 Nature of Work

My internship at Mandai was a beautiful fusion of technology and conservation. I got to work on Robotic Process Automation, GenAI applications, and creative storytelling through video production. The dynamic work environment enabled me to experience tasks across different domains—from back-end automation scripts to front-line animal feeding observations and interview logistics.

🚧 Challenges Faced

I encountered bugs in Power Automate Desktop, especially around web automation flows. GoPro mic syncing and vendor pricing complications also pushed us to think creatively under budget. Additionally, aligning schedules across multiple departments for interviews and updates taught me project and time management on a whole new level.

📈 Learning & Growth

I expanded my technical toolbox—learning Power Apps, AI Builder, Power BI, and prompt engineering. Beyond the tools, I built confidence in stakeholder communication, agile iteration, and adaptive problem-solving. I also earned 150+ certifications across RPA, cloud, AI, cybersecurity, and ethical design—solidifying my commitment to continuous learning.

🏆 Accomplishments

  • 🎬 Produced and presented “Project 100K” townhall video
  • 🤖 Delivered 3 functional Power Automate bots used by real departments
  • 📊 Conducted GenAI tourism analysis and visualised insights
  • 🦒 Contributed to field workflows through “Day in the Life” observation
  • 🏅 Completed 25+ certs from Microsoft, AWS, IBM, LinkedIn, AI Singapore & more

🚀 Career Aspirations

My future ambition is to become a cross-functional AI & RPA Engineer who can bridge user empathy with cutting-edge innovation. I aim to work on AI-powered applications for sustainability, conservation, and accessibility. Whether it's building smarter automation pipelines or designing inclusive AI tools, I want my work to help people and planet alike. 💚

🌟 Final Thoughts

This internship gave me the clarity and courage to shape my career path with confidence. I learnt to ask “why” before jumping to “how”, and to always connect every solution back to the people and impact it serves. Moving forward, I’m excited to take on larger challenges, refine my engineering skills, and always stay curious.
